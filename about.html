About me



I'm 16 and from Azerbaijan, but right now, I'm studying in China at UWC Changshu. My interest in computers started young, thanks to my dad's big PC at home. He worked as a journalist, and I was always curious about his computer. As I grew up, I got more into it, reading books about Computer Science and going to STEM camps. I got into a special school in Azerbaijan, Fizika, Riyaziyyat və Informatika Təmayüllü Respublika Liseyi, which was cool because they picked students who were into subjects like math and computer science. I learned a lot there and even took part in some informatics competitions. I also did this six-month course called Code&More, thanks to a Technest scholarship. Right now, I'm in China, still learning about computer science and working on a research article I hope to publish. My interest in environmental stuff started with a Disney series I watched with my sister. One episode, about DALL-E, really got me thinking. I joined a Green Club for a year, where we talked and did stuff to help the planet. Now, I'm part of the Sustainability Council at my school, even leading a team that organizes sustainability hackathons. I write articles about sustainability too and run a website, NaturEra, where I share my thoughts on how to make the world a better place. I want to use technology to solve big problems in the world. I'm thinking about starting my own projects and maybe becoming an entrepreneur. But I also have fun stuff I like to do – I play guitar and tar, enjoy basketball and football, and like drawing, especially people and landscapes. I made this website to share my ideas about tech and self-improvement and to let you know more about me. It's a place for honest thoughts and creating a friendly space.
